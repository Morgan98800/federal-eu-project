Chronology of
European Integration

A chronological record of significant moments in European federalist thought and institutional integration. Each entry links to its primary source.

1941

The Ventotene Manifesto

Written in secret on the island of Ventotene by Altiero Spinelli and Ernesto Rossi — both detained under Fascist confinement — the manifesto argued that the nation-state system had become the root cause of European wars and could only be transcended through a federal union. It circulated clandestinely through the Italian resistance and became the founding document of the European federalist movement.

Primary source: CVCE.eu — The Ventotene Manifesto (1941)

9 May 1950

The Schuman Declaration

French Foreign Minister Robert Schuman, drawing on a text drafted by Jean Monnet, proposed pooling European coal and steel production under a common High Authority — making war between France and Germany "not merely unthinkable, but materially impossible." The declaration led directly to the Treaty of Paris (1951) establishing the European Coal and Steel Community, the first supranational European institution. 9 May is now marked annually as Europe Day.

Primary source: European Commission — Schuman Declaration (1950)

25 March 1957

Treaty of Rome

Signed by Belgium, France, Italy, Luxembourg, the Netherlands, and West Germany, the Treaty of Rome established the European Economic Community (EEC), creating a common market and customs union. It committed signatories to "an ever closer union among the peoples of Europe" — a phrase that has anchored federalist interpretation of European law ever since.

Primary source: EUR-Lex — Treaty of Rome (EEC Treaty, 1957)

14 February 1984

Spinelli Draft Treaty on European Union

Altiero Spinelli, by then a member of the European Parliament, led the Crocodile Club — an informal grouping of MEPs — to draft a Treaty on European Union going substantially further than any existing Community text in the direction of a federal constitution. The Parliament adopted the draft by 237 votes to 31. Though never ratified by member states, it directly shaped the Single European Act (1986) and the Maastricht Treaty (1992).

Primary source: CVCE.eu — Draft Treaty on European Union (1984)

7 February 1992

Treaty of Maastricht

The Treaty on European Union, signed at Maastricht, created the European Union as a political entity, introduced EU citizenship, established the framework for Economic and Monetary Union, and extended qualified majority voting in the Council. It represented the most significant integration step since the Treaty of Rome. The Danish electorate initially rejected it (50.7% against, June 1992 referendum) before approving it in May 1993 after the Edinburgh Agreement secured four opt-outs.

Primary source: EUR-Lex — Treaty on European Union (Maastricht, 1992)

13 December 2007

Treaty of Lisbon

Signed following the failure of the Constitutional Treaty (rejected in French and Dutch referendums in 2005), the Lisbon Treaty gave the EU its current legal and institutional structure: a permanent European Council President, a strengthened European Parliament with co-legislative powers, a legally binding Charter of Fundamental Rights, and the Article 50 withdrawal mechanism. It entered into force on 1 December 2009.

Primary source: EUR-Lex — Treaty of Lisbon (2007)

September 2010

Spinelli Group Founded

Named after Altiero Spinelli, the Spinelli Group was founded in September 2010 by MEPs from multiple political families (Liberal, Green, Social Democrat) committed to the goal of a federal European Union. Founding members included Guy Verhofstadt (ALDE), Andrew Duff (ALDE), and Daniel Cohn-Bendit (Greens). The group published A Fundamental Law of the European Union (2013) as a worked constitutional proposal.

Reference: The Spinelli Group — official site

9 May 2022

Conference on the Future of Europe — Final Report

A year-long participatory exercise involving citizen panels, national parliaments, and EU institutions delivered its final report on Europe Day 2022, containing 49 proposals and 326 measures. Many citizen panels explicitly recommended treaty change to strengthen EU competences. The European Parliament voted in June 2022 to convene an Article 48 Convention; the Council's subsequent inaction became itself a point of democratic controversy.

Primary source: CoFoE — Final Report (May 2022, PDF)
