A Federal Vision
for Europe

To secure its political agency and economic model in a multipolar world, Europe must transition from a treaty-based confederation to a sovereign federal republic. This project outlines the structural mechanisms, historical basis, and democratic necessity of that union.

Sources informing this thesis

  • Sergio Fabbrini (2015), Which European Union?: Fabbrini argues that the EU's current intergovernmental framework is structurally inadequate for managing modern crises, advocating instead for a formal compound federal democracy to restore both efficiency and democratic legitimacy.
  • R. Daniel Kelemen (2004), The Rules of Federalism: Kelemen's comparative work demonstrates that the EU already functions as a federal system in its regulatory and legal structures, making the transition to a political federation the logical next step for securing its sovereignty.
  • Altiero Spinelli & Ernesto Rossi (1941), The Ventotene Manifesto: The foundational text arguing that absolute national sovereignty inevitably leads to political impotence and conflict, establishing federalism as a prerequisite for European security.
